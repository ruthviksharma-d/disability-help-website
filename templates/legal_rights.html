Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016

A Landmark Legislation

The RPWD Act 2016 replaces the Persons with Disabilities Act 1995, expanding the definition of disabilities from 7 to 21 types and strengthening rights and entitlements.

Your Fundamental Rights

Right to Equality

Equal treatment and non-discrimination in all spheres of life including employment, education, and access to goods and services.

  • Equal opportunities in employment
  • Non-discrimination in education
  • Equal access to public services

Right to Education

Free and compulsory education up to 18 years with reasonable accommodations and inclusive education.

  • Free education up to age 18
  • Reasonable accommodations in exams
  • Accessible infrastructure

Right to Accessibility

Access to physical environment, transportation, information and communication technologies.

  • Barrier-free access to buildings
  • Accessible public transport
  • Information in accessible formats

Right to Independent Living

Choose where and with whom to live, access to community support services and personal assistance.

  • Community living options
  • Personal assistance services
  • Support for independent living

Required Documentation

Disability Certificate

Issued by competent medical authority certifying minimum 40% disability

Valid for lifetime for permanent disabilities

UDID Card

Unique Disability Identity Card for unified access to benefits

Apply online at www.swavlambancard.gov.in

Medical Records

Supporting medical documents and assessment reports

Required for initial assessment and periodic reviews

Identity & Address Proof

Aadhaar card, passport, or other government-issued ID

Required for all government schemes and benefits

Complaint Redressal Mechanism

State Disability Commissioner

Each state has a Commissioner for Persons with Disabilities to address grievances and ensure implementation of the Act.

Powers & Functions:

  • • Investigate complaints
  • • Monitor implementation of schemes
  • • Take suo-moto cognizance
  • • Recommend corrective measures

Special Courts

Special courts designated for speedy trial of offences under the RPWD Act 2016.

Jurisdiction:

  • • Discrimination cases
  • • Violation of rights
  • • Accessibility non-compliance
  • • Denial of reasonable accommodation

How to File a Complaint

Complaints can be filed with the State Commissioner within 2 months of the incident. Extensions may be granted for sufficient cause.
